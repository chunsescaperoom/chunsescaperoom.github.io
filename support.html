This Was Never a Game

An experience that cannot be played — only preserved.

This is the closest you will ever get.

What is this?

This is not a game you can play.

It is a 70-minute immersive experience designed to simulate the subjective reality of PTSD.

Players believe they are in a war.

Only at the very end do they realize:

They were never in a battlefield.

They were inside a memory.

Why does this exist?

This experience exists physically in Taiwan.

Due to geographical limitations, most people will never be able to experience it.

So instead of letting it disappear, this page exists to preserve it.

Why support this?

Because some experiences deserve to exist — even if not everyone can access them.
